ABOUT THIS PROJECT

Capturing a city's transition

In the spring of 2026, the Municipality of The Hague organized multiple evening presentations on how they aim to make the city natural gas-free. Our studio was asked to attend several evenings, film the sessions, and edit them into an atmospheric impression. I was responsible for editing one of these videos.

The goal was to convey the energy, dialogue, and civic engagement of these gatherings — not as dry documentation, but as a cinematic piece that reflects both the urgency and hopefulness of the city's climate ambitions. The final edit balances spoken moments, ambient visuals, and a subtle narrative arc.

This project sits at the intersection of documentary filmmaking and civic storytelling. It required a restrained, empathetic hand — letting the room breathe while shaping a coherent emotional journey. The result is a sfeerimpressie (atmosphere impression) that served as both an internal record and a public-facing piece for the municipality.
